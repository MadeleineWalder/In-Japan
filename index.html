Introduction

My first trip to Japan was incredible. During a roughly two week stay in Japan I was mesmerized by how different it was compared to back home in Europe. It was such a cool experience that I never wanted to forget. In an attempt to explain in words what Japan is really like I ended up writing a list of little things that are different over there. This list quickly grew a lot larger than I was expecting, so now it has become a blog I guess! It will also be a great excuse to use some of the best photos we took. I hope you enjoy!

CLEAN!

This is something I had heard about before going to Japan but I didn't think much of it until I was there. It is SO hard to find a bin. It was pretty annoying for people like us who were out all day everyday buying food and drinks as we went. Every time we bought street food (which was many many times) we would be keeping an eye out for a bin for hours afterwards. Yet despite this the streets are very clean. In contrast to Europe it seems that the bigger the city you're in the cleaner it is. And not just when it comes to litter. Public bathrooms and transport were incredibly clean too. You get the feeling people greatly respect each other and the environment. Especially when combined with small things like keeping to one side on the stairs, queuing in specific marked lines, muting your phone on public transport and the airport ground staff waving at the planes as they take off.

Foooood

You know it's going to be good when the staff don't speak English. Having to point to things on the menu and then show with our fingers how many of it we wanted was funny. Especially the hole-in-the-wall type ones. Some restaurants are literally people's garages, or extensions of their houses. Some only fit three people! A street can look abandoned during the day but at night everything opens up into busy restaurants. Sometimes we didn't really know what we ordered but it didn't matter. If it was any type of sushi or ramen it was going to be better than we'd ever had at home. It's super common to eat out after work as the food is so cheap. Lots of people eat alone as they don't go out to eat for a specific occasion, it's just casual to get some food on the way home.

We could afford to eat out three meals a day, every day, because food is so much cheaper. Getting breakfast at a convenience store was amazing. They would have fresh hash browns, pork buns and more every moring. And you could always buy instant ramen and cook it in the store. Even the smallest stores had hot water and a place to sit. Noodles are always accessible. Onigiri are my favorite snack and I just can't make them the same as in Japan. You get used to eating everything with chopsticks, even rice which is surprisingly easy. The rice there is somehow so much better. And the fish. When we got home it felt so weird to hold a knife and fork again. But it's kind of necessary in order to cut your food, wheras in Japan most things are already bite-size.
